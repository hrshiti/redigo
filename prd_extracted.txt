EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This document is the formal Product Requirement Document (PRD) for Redigo, a unified mobility and local
logistics super-app targeting the underserved tier-2 and tier-3 city market in India, with Indore, Madhya Pradesh
as the launch market. Redigo is designed to solve a fragmented local transportation and delivery landscape by
consolidating six distinct service verticals into a single, seamless digital platform.
The platform will serve daily commuters, students, small business owners, and local travelers by providing
affordable, hyper-local access to bike taxis, auto and cab booking, self-drive bike rentals, intercity travel,
spiritual and temple tour packages, parcel delivery, and a differentiated cab-sharing feature with real-time seat
visualization. A robust Admin Panel enables the platform operator to manage users, drivers, pricing, packages,
and analytics from a centralized web interface.
The backend is built on the MERN stack (MongoDB, Express.js, React.js, Node.js) with a React Native or Flutter
mobile client. Real-time functionality is delivered via Socket.io. Payments are processed through Razorpay,
authentication via Firebase OTP, and location services via Google Maps API. This PRD defines all functional
modules, user personas, architecture, technology decisions, integration requirements, security measures,
monetization logic, and phased delivery plan.

PRODUCT VISION & MISSION STATEMENT
Redigo's product vision is to become the default mobility and logistics layer for tier-2 and tier-3 Indian cities ù
markets that are currently underserved by national aggregators like Ola, Uber, and Rapido, which are primarily
focused on metro and tier-1 expansion. These smaller cities have dense daily commuter populations, growing
smartphone penetration, and a strong cultural preference for local, affordable, and trustworthy services.
Vision Statement: "One app for everything travel and delivery in local India ù fast, affordable, and hyper-local. Built for
Indore. Designed for Bharat."
The platform is built on four core strategic pillars:
Hyper-Local Focus: Built ground-up for Indore and designed to expand city by city with local driver networks,
local pricing intelligence, and culturally relevant features such as temple tour packages unique to the Malwa and
Nimar belt of Madhya Pradesh.

MARKET CONTEXT & OPPORTUNITY
India's mobility market is one of the largest and fastest-growing in the world, yet the vast majority of investment,
innovation, and product development has been concentrated in tier-1 metro cities. Tier-2 and tier-3 cities like
Indore, Bhopal, Nagpur, Surat, and Coimbatore represent a combined population of over 300 million people with
rapidly rising smartphone adoption, growing middle-class income, and an acute shortage of reliable, app-based
local mobility solutions.
3.1 Why Indore as the Launch Market
Indore is the commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh with a population exceeding 3.5 million, making it one of
the largest tier-2 cities in central India.
The city has been ranked India's cleanest city for seven consecutive years, reflecting strong civic
engagement and a growing urban middle class.
Home to a large student population (IIM Indore, IIT Indore, DAVV, and numerous colleges) representing an
ideal high-frequency, price-sensitive user segment for bike taxis and cab sharing.
Indore serves as the nearest major gateway city for pilgrimage destinations including Ujjain (Mahakal
Jyotirlinga) and Omkareshwar ù making spiritual travel packages a high-demand, low-competition niche.
Existing app-based mobility options in Indore are limited ù Ola and Uber have inconsistent availability
outside core urban areas, and Rapido's bike taxi presence is nascent.
Strong local SME and retail economy generating consistent demand for same-day intra-city parcel delivery.

6.2 Booking Flow
Step 1 ù Location Input: User sets pickup location (auto-detected via GPS or manually entered) and drop
location via Google Maps autocomplete.
Step 2 ù Vehicle Selection: App displays available vehicle types with estimated fare, estimated arrival time,

Step 3 ù Booking Confirmation: User confirms booking. Driver receives ride request with 30-second accept

booking time.
Ride History: Complete history of all past rides including route map, fare breakdown, driver name, and rating

CORE MODULE 3 ù INTERCITY TRAVEL
The Intercity Travel module provides users with the ability to book dedicated cab rides between cities. Unlike the
local ride booking module, intercity trips are pre-scheduled, distance-based, and serve both individual travelers
and family groups requiring a complete vehicle for a longer journey. This module competes directly with local
cab operators who rely on phone-based bookings with no digital tracking or price transparency.

City-to-City Route Selection: User selects origin and destination city from a predefined list covering major

Scheduled Booking: All intercity trips are pre-scheduled. User selects travel date and preferred departure
time (morning / afternoon / flexible).
Vehicle Type Selection: Mini cab, Sedan, or SUV options displayed with fare and passenger capacity for

history.
8.3 Pricing Engine
Intercity fares are calculated using a pre-configured rate card stored in the Admin Panel. The formula combines
a fixed base fare (covering initial distance and driver pickup costs) with a per-kilometer rate applied to the total
route distance. Admin can create and maintain the rate card for each origin-destination pair and update it
without a code change.

The Parcel Delivery module enables users to send parcels within the city (intra-city) and between cities
(intercity) using the same driver and vehicle network that serves the ride-booking module. This creates asset
utilization efficiency ù vehicles that are idle between rides can fulfill delivery assignments. The module targets
small business owners, e-commerce sellers, and individuals who need reliable, trackable same-day delivery.

10.2 Booking Flow
Step 1 ù Parcel Details: User enters parcel weight (weight brackets: up to 2kg, 2û5kg, 5û10kg, 10kg+),
dimensions, and item category (documents / fragile / general goods).
Step 2 ù Pickup & Drop: User sets pickup address and delivery address. For intercity, user selects

Step 3 ù Receiver Details: User enters receiver name and phone number. The receiver is notified via
SMS/app notification when the parcel is picked up.
Step 4 ù Fare Estimate: System calculates fare based on weight bracket, distance, and delivery type.
Breakdown shown transparently before payment.
Step 5 ù Payment: User pays via in-app wallet, UPI, or cash on pickup.
Step 6 ù Driver Assignment: Nearest available delivery agent is assigned. Agent receives pickup address
and parcel details.
Step 7 ù Pickup: Agent arrives at pickup address. User hands over parcel. Agent captures parcel photo

Step 8 ù Live Tracking: Both sender and receiver can track the parcel's live location until delivery.

delivery agent to confirm receipt.
Step 10 ù Delivery Confirmation: System marks delivery complete. Sender receives confirmation notification
with delivery timestamp.
10.3 Pricing Logic
Parcel pricing is calculated using a weight-bracket base rate combined with a per-kilometer distance charge.
The formula is: Base Rate (by weight bracket) + (Distance in km x Per-km Rate). Additional surcharges apply for
fragile items, declared high-value parcels, and time-specific deliveries. All rates are managed via the Admin
Pricing Panel and can be updated without code changes.

CORE MODULE 6 ù CAB SHARING (USP FEATURE)
Cab Sharing is Redigo's primary product differentiator. No competitor in the Indore market currently offers real-time
seat-based ride sharing with live visual seat maps. This module is positioned as the platform's flagship feature and is
expected to drive the highest frequency of social sharing, word-of-mouth growth, and media coverage at launch.
The Cab Sharing module allows multiple unrelated users to book individual seats in a single vehicle traveling
along a common route ù splitting the cost of the ride proportionally. A real-time visual seat layout shows users
which seats are already booked and which are available before they confirm their booking.
11.1 How Cab Sharing Works
A driver (or the platform on behalf of a driver) creates a shared ride trip with a defined route (e.g., Vijay
Nagar to Rajwada, Indore), departure time, number of available seats, and per-seat price.
The trip appears in the Cab Sharing section of the user app for users whose origin and destination are within
the defined route corridor.
Users can view the trip details: route on map, departure time, driver details, per-seat price, and a real-time
visual seat layout showing available and booked seats.
User selects their preferred seat from the visual layout and books it via payment.
As each seat is booked, the visual layout updates in real time for all other users browsing the same trip using
Socket.io.
When all seats are filled (or the trip departs), the trip is marked as full and removed from the available
listings.
Driver picks up all confirmed passengers from their individual pickup points along the route.
Each passenger pays their per-seat fare independently. No single user bears the cost of unfilled seats.

11.3 Dynamic Pricing Logic
Base per-seat price is set by the driver or the platform when the trip is created.
As seat occupancy increases, per-seat price decreases slightly for remaining seats ù rewarding early

SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE
Redigo is built on a service-oriented backend with a shared API layer serving all three client interfaces. The
architecture is designed for horizontal scalability to handle concurrent ride requests, real-time Socket.io
connections, and high-frequency database writes from GPS tracking events.
Mobile Client Layer: React Native (or Flutter) cross-platform mobile application serving both iOS and Android
from a single codebase. Separate app builds for User App and Driver App. Google Maps SDK embedded for
in-app mapping and navigation.
Admin Web Layer: React.js single-page application (SPA) styled with Tailwind CSS. Desktop-first layout
optimized for management workflows. Served as a static build via Nginx. Role-protected routes enforced by
JWT middleware.
API Gateway / Backend Layer: Node.js + Express.js REST API layer organized into domain modules: Auth,
Rides, Rentals, Intercity, Parcels, SpiritualTrips, CabSharing, Users, Drivers, Pricing, Notifications, Analytics. All
routes protected by JWT authentication. Role-based guards per route.
Real-Time Layer: Socket.io server running alongside the Express API for bidirectional real-time events: live
driver GPS updates, cab sharing seat status changes, booking notifications, parcel tracking updates, and OTP
events.
Database Layer: MongoDB Atlas (cloud-hosted) as the primary database. Collections designed for each
domain entity. Mongoose ODM for schema enforcement. Indexed on geo-coordinates (2dsphere index) for
location queries, and on frequently queried fields (status, userId, driverId, createdAt).
Infrastructure Layer: Deployed on AWS EC2 (or equivalent VPS). Nginx as reverse proxy for both
HTTP/REST and WebSocket traffic. PM2 for Node.js process management. SSL/TLS via Let's Encrypt. CI/CD
pipeline for automated deployments. MongoDB Atlas handles database replication and automated backups.

UI/UX DESIGN GUIDELINES
Redigo's visual identity and user experience are designed to feel simultaneously modern and locally familiar.
The design language balances the clean efficiency expected of a tech product with warmth and accessibility
appropriate for the tier-2 Indian demographic, including users with limited prior experience of app-based mobility
services.
Color System: Primary: Orange (#E85D04) ù energy, action, movement, Indian warmth. Secondary: Amber
(#F48C06) ù highlights, warm CTAs, icons. Neutral: White (#FFFFFF) for backgrounds, Dark Gray (#1C2833)
for primary text. Success/Error states use standard green (#10B981) and red (#EF4444). The orange-dominant
palette is culturally resonant in Madhya Pradesh and differentiates Redigo from the black-and-yellow of Ola or
the blue of Uber.
Typography: Primary typeface: Inter or Poppins (clean, high legibility on mobile screens at small sizes). Hindi
language support via Noto Sans Devanagari for all Hindi-language interface strings. Font sizes follow an 8-point
scale. Minimum body text 14sp on mobile.
Booking Speed: The booking flow for any service must be completable in 3 taps or fewer from the home
screen. Home screen features large, icon-driven service cards for instant navigation. The most recent booking
type is remembered and pre-selected on next app open.
Map-First Design: The default home screen state centers on a full-screen map view showing the user's location
and nearby drivers. Service selection overlays appear from the bottom as sheets rather than navigating away
from the map context. This creates a spatially grounded experience.
Hindi Language Toggle: The app includes a Hindi/English language toggle accessible from the profile screen.
All UI strings, package descriptions, and booking confirmations are translated into Hindi for the toggle state.
Driver communication suggestions also appear in Hindi.
Animation & Motion: Framer Motion (React Native Reanimated on mobile) for screen transitions and
gesture-driven interactions. Lottie animations for loading states, booking confirmation, and empty states. Driver
icon on map uses smooth interpolated movement rather than jumping between GPS coordinates.
Accessibility: Minimum touch target size of 44x44pt for all interactive elements. Sufficient color contrast ratios
per WCAG AA standards. Screen reader labels on all icon-only buttons.

SECURITY & COMPLIANCE
User Authentication: Firebase Phone Auth with OTP verification for all user registrations and logins. OTPs
expire in 60 seconds. Accounts can be suspended on admin command.
API Authentication: All backend API endpoints protected by JWT Bearer token middleware. Tokens expire in
24 hours with a refresh token mechanism. Role guards enforce admin vs. user vs. driver access per endpoint.
Driver KYC Verification: All drivers must submit: Driving License (front and back), Vehicle RC, Insurance
Certificate, PUC Certificate, and a live selfie. Admin manually verifies all documents before the driver goes live.
Periodic re-verification scheduled.
Payment Security: All payment processing via Razorpay's PCI-DSS compliant infrastructure. No raw card data
is ever stored on Redigo servers. Refunds and payouts handled via Razorpay's payout API with audit logs.
Data Security: All sensitive fields (passwords where applicable) hashed with bcrypt. Personally identifiable
information (PII) fields encrypted at rest. Database access restricted to application server IPs.
Transport Security: HTTPS enforced across all API and WebSocket connections. SSL/TLS via Let's Encrypt
with auto-renewal. HTTP to HTTPS redirect at Nginx level with HSTS headers.
User Safety Features: In-ride SOS button sharing live location with emergency contacts. Ride sharing: both
rider and driver share masked phone numbers via platform relay to protect PII. Trip sharing: users can share live
trip link with family/friends.
Data Retention & Privacy: User data retained per applicable Indian IT Act and DPDPA (Digital Personal Data
Protection Act) requirements. Users can request account deletion, which anonymizes personal data while
retaining transactional records for compliance.

The Redigo platform is delivered across three sequential development phases. Each phase builds on the
previous and represents a functional, testable milestone. No timeline is committed in this document ù sprint
duration will be scoped separately during the project kick-off based on final team composition and resource
allocation.
Phase 1 ù MVP ù Core Ride & Delivery Launch
Phase 1 establishes the foundational platform with the two highest-demand service verticals. The goal is to get a
working, tested, and deployable product into the market as quickly as possible to begin acquiring users,
onboarding drivers, and generating real-world operational data.
User App: Ride booking (Bike, Auto, Mini Cab, Sedan) with live GPS tracking and OTP ride start.
User App: Parcel delivery (intra-city) with weight-based pricing, live tracking, and OTP verification.
Driver App: Ride and delivery assignment, navigation, status updates, OTP handling.
Admin Panel: Core dashboard with live rides and driver map, user management, driver management with
KYC review, and basic pricing configuration.
Authentication: Firebase OTP login for users and drivers. JWT-secured API.
Payments: Razorpay integration for ride and delivery payments (UPI, cards).
Notifications: Push notifications for booking confirmation, driver assigned, and ride status.
Google Maps: Full integration for pickup/drop selection, fare estimation, and live tracking.
Phase 2 ù Advanced Verticals & Payments Expansion
Phase 2 expands the platform to include three additional service verticals and deepens the payment,
notification, and analytics capabilities. At this stage, Redigo becomes a true multi-vertical platform and the
monetization model fully activates.
User App: Bike rental module with DL upload, availability calendar, security deposit via Razorpay, and fleet
owner listing.
User App: Intercity travel module with route selection, scheduled booking, vehicle choice, and advance
payment.

request form.
Driver App: Enhancements for intercity trip support (pre-trip notification, multi-day tracking).
Vendor Portal: Web interface for travel vendors to create, manage, and track spiritual tour package bookings.
Admin Panel: Full Pricing Control Panel (surge pricing, intercity rate card, rental pricing, commission rates).
Admin Panel: Package Management module for spiritual tours (create, approve, edit, track).
Analytics Dashboard: Revenue by vertical, DAU/MAU, driver utilization, completion rates.
Wallet System: In-app credit wallet with top-up via Razorpay and auto-deduction on bookings.
Intercity Parcel: Extend parcel delivery module to support intercity shipments.
